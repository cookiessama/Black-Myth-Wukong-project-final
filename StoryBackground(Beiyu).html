Black Myth: Wukong

The Gamesworld

Settings

The game is inspired by the classical Chinese novel Journey to the West and follows an anthropomorphic monkey based on Sun Wukong from the novel.

It has six chapters, each focusing on a specific location: Black Wind Mountain, Yellow Wind Ridge, The New West, The Webbed Hollow, Flaming Mountains, and Mount Huaguo.

The game is set in a world with gods from the Chinese pantheon, including Buddhist, Daoist, and other deities. Many yaoguais (monsters), who are beings with preternatural abilities and strange appearances, inhabit this world.

The game introduces cultural and natural landmarks derived from the real world into its setting. It features, for example, several locations from Shanxi Province, such as the Yungang Grottoes, Little Western Paradise, Nanchan Temple, Iron Buddha Temple, Guangsheng Temple, and Stork Tower, as well as locations from other provinces, such as the Kaiyuan Temple in Fujian Province.

Each chapter concludes with an animated cutscene providing a reflection and exploration of the narrative and thematic elements of Black Myth Wukong. For instance, the first three cutscenes resonate with Chinese philosophy, while the fourth cutscene relates to a universal theme of love.

Story Line

After escorting his master Tang Sanzang and retrieving the Buddhist scriptures, Sun Wukong ascends to Buddhahood but chooses a simple life on Mount Huaguo over the Celestial Court, which leads to his imprisonment in stone by Erlang Shen. Centuries later, a monkey known as "The Destined One" sets out to recover six relics scattered across the mortal realm to revive Wukong. Along his journey, he faces various foes, including the Black Bear Guai, Yellow Wind Sage, and Red Boy, while gathering relics with the help of allies like Zhu Bajie. Ultimately, after confronting Erlang Shen and obtaining Wukong's armor, the Destined One learns that he must inherit Wukong's legacy rather than restore him entirely. In the end, he either becomes trapped in stone like Wukong or retains his memories and freedom, depending on the choices made during the adventure.

Characters

The Destined One

The Destined One is the titular main protagonist of Black Myth: Wukong.

An anthropomorphic monkey from Mount Huaguo, he is one of many young warriors who embark on a journey to revive the legendary Monkey King Sun Wukong by recovering six relics: the Six Senses of the Great Sage that Wukong had split into following his defeat centuries ago, which have scattered across the lands of China and India.

Near the end of the game, it is revealed that the Destined One is one of the relics: the Mind of Sun Wukong, who is meant to be his new incarnation.

Sun Wukong

Sun Wukong ("Monkey Awakened to the Void/Emptiness"), also known as the Monkey King, is a literary and religious figure best known as one of the main characters from the popular 16th-century Chinese novel, Journey to the West. In the novel, Sun Wukong is a monkey born from a stone who acquires supernatural powers through Taoist practices. After rebelling against heaven, he is imprisoned under a mountain by the Buddha. Five hundred years later, he accompanies the monk Tang Sanzang riding on the White Dragon Horse and two other disciples, Zhu Bajie and Sha Wujing, on a journey to obtain Buddhist sutras from India, known as the West or Western Paradise, where Buddha and his followers dwell.

Appearing as the player's character during the prologue and opening fight of Black Myth: Wukong, whereafter Wukong's fated successor, the Destined One, becomes the game's playable protagonist. His broken, senseless, and instinct-driven corpse becomes the final boss for Chapter 6: Unfinished and overall, the game itself.

Erlang Shen

Erlang Shen is a Yaoguai King in Black Myth: Wukong. During the fight with the Destined One, Erlang, the Sacred Divinity transforms into Erlang Shen.

Erlang, the Sacred Divinity

Erlang, the Sacred Divinity is a Yaoguai King and the overarching antihero of Black Myth: Wukong. He is a mandatory boss fought as an opening battle during the game's prologue, as well as an optional boss that the player must defeat if they want to achieve the secret ending (True Ending), where he turns into Erlang Shen.

He is a powerful three-eyed, spear-wielding warrior god from Heaven associated with flood control, justice, warriorhood, hunting, and demon subdual. He, alongside Nezha, is the archrival of the Monkey King, and the bearer of the sixth and final relic of Wukong as revealed towards the end of the game.
